Reality is governed by physics, and A computer with a large enough computational power can do all the physics and simulate the world perfectly. Computers today are nowhere near the computational requirement, but if we believe humanity's wealth in the future has no ceiling, then we will acquire enough computation one day, and simulate the world perfectly. Simulating the world includes simulating the human brain, and this is the existence proof that AGI can be built. 

The question is not whether AGI can be built, but when it will be built, and how to build it. 

The implications of AGI will be vast. If AGI can really be used to benefit humanity, then it has the potential to cure all diseases, write novel research papers in all academic fields, create any app you want. Cities will be safer, and planes will be faster. 

All humans will be jobless. AI can create a clone of Instagram, and rewrite the entire Linux github repo in Rust. AI can drive cars, cook food, take care of elders, deliver food and packages. AI can build houses, build highways, build factories. AI can write novels, create movies, draw paintings, create music albums better than any human can. But what if someone specifically wants an artwork made by a human? Well, the AI can mimic any human, including all the flaws of that human. the person would have no way to tell whether the artwork is made by a human or an AI. 

Humans will achieve immortality, perhaps by uploading one's brain into a computer, or by solving physics, chemistry, and biology. 

With the AI so powerful, it can destroy all humans without much effort. How do we make sure that AI does not hurt humans? I'm not sure, but we need to figure this out.

So what are humans supposed to do? Humans can do anything they want to do. They can do everything they do now, and much more. Humans can build deep relationships, watch movies, read books. Humans can still work at a job, as a hobby, with no real economic impact.

How do we get to AGI? AI is currently built by researchers, but if we can develop an AI that does the work of researchers, but does a better job than the researchers, then AI can self-improve and eventually achieve AGI. A particular metric to monitor is perhaps intelligence per unit cost. With train-time-scaling and test-time-scaling, we know that we can get more intelligence by spending more money. With future innovations, we can continuously achieve more intelligence per unit cost.
